# Prologue: Echoes of Light

The signal reached the Chamber three hundred million kilometers before the ship did.

It arrived not as sound or light but as a disturbance in the crystalline lattice at the core of the structure — a structure that had been waiting inside the red planet for longer than the planet had been red. The lattice had recorded the first bacterial colonies in Martian soil. It had registered the slow death of the magnetic field, the stripping of atmosphere, the long silence that followed. It had waited through epochs that would break any human calendar.

Now something was approaching. The lattice shifted. Patterns moved across its surface — constellations rearranging themselves into configurations that corresponded to no sky visible from Mars. Among them, one formation burned brighter than the rest: seven stars in the shape of a hunter, carrying a question the Chamber had heard before, from other species, on other worlds, in the time before this solar system had condensed from dust.

*What are we becoming?*

The ship was still beyond Jupiter's orbit when the Chamber began to respond. Deep beneath the Valles Marineris, bioluminescent walls pulsed once — a heartbeat after geological ages of stillness. Glyphs older than terrestrial language shifted on curved surfaces. The crystalline core, suspended at zero gravity in its spherical housing, emitted a frequency that would take months to reach the vessel but would arrive precisely when needed.

The Orion carried ten thousand humans toward Mars. They had been selected, sorted, optimized. They called themselves the Chosen, and they carried with them every sickness that had driven them from their dying world — hierarchy dressed as efficiency, control calling itself vision, fear of each other renamed as prudence.

They believed they were flying toward an empty planet.

The Chamber knew better. It had been listening for them since before their species learned to speak.
